CFL discussion sheet

Lost Lake, Phase 1 Discussion Point

The lake house is currently a significant drain on family resources. While a sale may ultimately become the long-term outcome, that is a slower and separate process. In the meantime, there is a practical interim option worth advancing.

Core framing

The immediate objective is not to debate the full Lost Lake concept in detail. The immediate objective is to determine whether the property can help reduce its current cost burden and whether a short-term rental model is actually viable.

Phase 1 proposal

Phase 1 would be a limited short-term rental test using a controlled rollout. The purpose is to move from theory to evidence without overcommitting to the larger concept.

The goals of Phase 1 are straightforward

  • reduce family income requirements by offsetting some of the property expense with revenue
  • test whether the short-term rental concept is viable
  • assess both profitability and logistics in real-world conditions
  • create a factual basis for any future discussion

Why this approach makes sense

Right now, the property continues to consume resources whether or not it is being actively evaluated. A limited Phase 1 approach creates a practical interim path.

Rather than arguing the full concept before there is evidence, this approach allows the property to be tested in a measured way.

If the test works, there is a basis for discussing future phases. If the test does not work, then there is little reason to carry the larger concept forward.

What Phase 1 is not

  • not a final commitment to the full Lost Lake Resort concept
  • not a replacement for long-term sale discussions
  • not an attempt to overbuild the idea before viability is proven

What Phase 1 is

  • a controlled operating test
  • a practical way to reduce financial drag
  • a method for testing market response and operating reality
  • an evidence-based step that supports better future decisions

Discussion point for CFL

The key question is not whether to approve the entire long-term concept today. The key question is whether it makes sense to use a limited Phase 1 trial to determine if the property can offset expense and prove short-term rental viability while longer-term decisions continue on their own timeline.
Bottom line This is a practical interim strategy designed to reduce ongoing drain, test viability, gather evidence, and support better long-term decisions.
