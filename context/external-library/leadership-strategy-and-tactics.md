# Leadership Strategy and Tactics: Field Manual
**Author:** Jocko Willink

## Core Philosophy
A practical guide that translates military leadership into civilian/business execution. It is built on taking absolute accountability and removing ego from the chain of command.

## Key Frameworks & Principles

### 1. Extreme Ownership
- The leader is responsible for *everything* in their domain.
- Own all problems, mistakes, and failed plans. No excuses, no blaming the team.

### 2. The Dichotomy of Leadership
- Leadership requires balancing opposing forces:
  - Discipline vs. flexibility
  - Confidence vs. humility
  - Aggressive execution vs. hands-off delegation
  - Speaking up vs. staying quiet

### 3. Four Laws of Combat
- **Cover and Move:** Teams must support each other. It is one overall mission.
- **Simple:** Keep plans clear and straightforward. If it gets complex, simplify it.
- **Prioritize and Execute:** In chaos, pick the single most critical task, execute it, then move to the next.
- **Decentralized Command:** Empower everyone on the team to lead. Give them the "why" so they can act independently when things change.

### 4. Key Tactics
- **Detachment:** Step back physically and mentally to see the bigger picture. Avoid tunnel vision.
- **Check Your Ego:** Arrogance prevents growth. Do not micromanage.
- **Lead by Example:** Embody the standard you expect.
- **Iterative Decision-Making:** In stressful environments, make small, adaptable choices rather than massive, irreversible commitments.
