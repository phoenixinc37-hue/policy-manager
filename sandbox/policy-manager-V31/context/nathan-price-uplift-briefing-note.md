# Nathan Briefing Note
## Price Uplift Campaign

Scott and Jack Wilde have developed a **Price Uplift Campaign** built around a simple operating idea: if pricing drift happens continuously, pricing review should happen continuously too.

Most clinics do not have a true ongoing pricing process. Fees are reviewed occasionally, some items are changed, others are missed, and over time prices drift behind inflation or strategic intent. The result is quiet revenue and profit leakage.

This campaign is designed to address that with **small, report-driven pricing recommendations** across services or products. It is not based on large disruptive jumps, and it is not built around instant implementation. Recommendations are generated from actual pricing data, reviewed logically, approved by leadership, and then implemented either by staff or through automation.

The monthly model matters. Rather than waiting for drift to accumulate and then forcing a larger catch-up increase, the default cadence is **monthly** so pricing gaps stay small and most adjustments remain modest and easier for teams and clients to absorb.

## Why the concept is interesting

This is not being framed as a one-time pricing exercise. It is a **pricing governance system** designed to reduce drift on an ongoing basis.

That distinction may matter in the current environment. In groups where prices have already been raised broadly, additional blunt increases can create resistance at the clinic level because front-line staff take the heat from clients. This model works in the opposite direction: **small, justified, supportable changes**, backed by reports and logic, rather than large blanket jumps.

It also keeps leadership in control. Nothing is pushed automatically. The system produces options, forecasts, and recommendations, and approved changes can then be executed internally or automated.

## What the campaign includes

### 1. Inflationary Uplift Initiative
A recurring review of services or products that have drifted behind inflation or have not been updated in an extended period. The purpose is to recommend modest, defensible increases before the gap becomes large enough to require a disruptive catch-up.

This makes pricing changes:
- smaller
- more logical
- easier to defend operationally
- easier for clients to absorb

### 2. 99 Cent Factor Initiative
A configurable pricing lever based on straightforward buyer psychology.

The logic is simple: **$8.34 often lands very similarly to $8.99 in the customer’s mind, even though the realized margin is different over time.**

Leadership can define the parameters, for example:
- all products over $8
- all products over $2
- OTC only
- pet food only
- selected categories only

Before approval, the system can estimate likely uplift using prior sales history so leadership can see the expected effect of each rule set.

## Strategic pricing angle

The campaign is not limited to inflation alignment. It can also be used strategically.

For example, a clinic may want to lower a **shoppable price** such as an office visit to stay competitive while recovering margin through associated services that commonly attach to that visit. The system can help identify those patterns and model the pricing needed to preserve economics.

That means the campaign can support both:
- competitive pricing strategy
- margin recovery strategy

## Evidence from clinic work

Current pricing review work identified meaningful opportunity:

- **Tudor Glen:** 251 service codes, approximately **$74,613** annual opportunity
- **River Valley:** 161 service codes, approximately **$27,337** annual opportunity
- **Rosslyn:** preliminary estimate of **$25,000 to $30,000**
- **Combined:** roughly **$130,000 annually**

The owner-level proof is especially visible in the pricing-age reports. Many items had gone **700+ to 1000+ days** without updates. Some had gone **2000+ to 4000+ days**. That makes the concept easy to grasp: drift is not theoretical, it is visible in the age of the fee schedule itself.

## Core message in one sentence

**The Price Uplift Campaign is a report-driven system that recommends small, logical price adjustments across services or products, avoids big jumps, requires approval before implementation, and can be executed by staff or through automation.**
