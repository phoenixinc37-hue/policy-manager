## Executive Summary

The veterinary AI and pricing software market is active and growing, but **no competitor is doing exactly what Clinic IQ does.** Most tools either show clinics what competitors charge (external benchmarking) or analyze overall financial performance. Clinic IQ's unique value is **internal forensic pricing** — finding the money you're already losing by looking at your own stale prices. That gap is real and unoccupied.

## Section 1 — Direct Competitors (Pricing Intelligence)

### 🔴 Sikka AI — Fee Survey
**Closest competitor. Know this one cold.**

- **What it does:** AI-powered fee survey that tells clinics what other practices in their zip code are charging. Uses 15 years of data, millions of transactions. Delivers percentile-based pricing (50th–90th percentile) for 900+ procedure codes.
- **Technology:** Vetcabulary™ — proprietary NLP that normalizes unstructured vet procedure descriptions into standardized categories. This is their moat.
- **Pricing:** $495 one-time purchase for a 3-zip-code snapshot. No subscription.
- **Also sells:** Fee Schedule Optimizer — AI recommendations for optimal fees based on practice style, frequency, and profitability.
- **Compatible with:** AVIMark, CornerStone, DVMAX, ezyVet, Impromed, and others.
- **Gap vs. Clinic IQ:** Sikka tells you what the market charges. Clinic IQ tells you what *you're* charging that you shouldn't be charging anymore. Different problem, different product.

**Threat level: MEDIUM.** They're in the space but solving a different problem. However, a clinic that buys a Sikka report might feel "done" on pricing — sales strategy needs to address this.

---

### 🟡 AAHA Veterinary Fee Reference
- **What it does:** Published guide from the American Animal Hospital Association. Data from 950+ practices, 500+ services. Median fees, regional breakdowns.
- **Pricing:** Paid publication, updated periodically. Not software.
- **Gap vs. Clinic IQ:** Static, not real-time. No implementation. No internal analysis.
- **Threat level: LOW.** Different format, different use case.

---

### 🟡 FareVet
- **What it does:** Consumer-facing price comparison tool. Helps pet owners compare vet bills and identify unusual charges.
- **Gap vs. Clinic IQ:** B2C, not B2B. Aimed at clients, not clinics.
- **Threat level: LOW** as a competitor. **Interesting as a future data source** if they build a B2B product.

## Section 4 — The Gap Clinic IQ Fills

Every competitor does one of three things:
1. **External benchmarking** — what competitors charge (Sikka, AAHA)
2. **Financial performance analytics** — how the clinic is performing overall (iVET360, VetSuccess, HappyDoc)
3. **Operational efficiency** — reducing admin time (Shepherd, Digitail, Covetrus)

**Nobody is doing internal pricing forensics** — analyzing a clinic's own historical invoice data to find prices that haven't been updated in 1, 2, or 3+ years, calculating the daily revenue loss, and providing a prioritized implementation path.

That is Clinic IQ's white space.

## Section 5 — Market Observations

**1. The market is ready.** Every source confirms that vet clinics are being told to update fees regularly but most don't have a system to do it. The problem is acknowledged industry-wide.

**2. Price sensitivity is rising.** Pet owner financial anxiety is increasing. Clinics feel pressure not to raise prices. Clinic IQ's message of "catch up to where you already should be" is more palatable than "charge more."

**3. IDEXX is the gorilla.** They own ezyVet, Neo, and have massive clinic penetration. If they build a pricing intelligence module, that's the biggest threat. Watch for it.

**4. DSOs (Dental/Vet Service Organizations) are consolidating.** Large corporate groups already have internal analytics teams. Clinic IQ's sweet spot is **independent clinics and small groups (1–5 locations)** — the ones who don't have a Deb or an iVET360 on retainer.

**5. The .99 Factor is still unique.** No competitor has identified or named the specific strategy of pricing just below round numbers as a standalone concept. That's IP worth protecting.

3. **Monitor Digitail's invoice AI** — if they expand to price-age analysis, that's the first real overlap.

## Section 7 — Sikka Deep Dive

**Scale:** 1,700 veterinary practices on the platform. 41M+ animal patients in their database. 15 years of data. This is not a startup — they have real depth.

**Their sales pitch (verbatim from their site):**
*"Our Fee Survey uses advanced AI to group similar procedures by location, comparing your pricing against regional benchmarks so you never overcharge or undercharge — supercharge your profits with data-driven fee recommendations."*

**Their core message:** Don't leave money on the table by charging below market. Don't lose clients by charging above market. Find the sweet spot.

**Their technology moat — Vetcabulary™:**
The real IP. Vet medicine has no standardized procedure codes (unlike dental or medical). Sikka built an NLP system that normalizes inconsistent, unstructured procedure descriptions across thousands of practices — turning "spay female cat 6 months" and "feline OHE <1yr" into the same category. This is genuinely hard to replicate. It took 15 years of data to build.

**Their product suite:**
1. **Fee Survey** — $495 one-time, 3 zip codes, 900+ procedure codes, percentile breakdown (50th–90th)
2. **Optimizer™** — real-time practice dashboards, metrics tracking
3. **Marketplace** — business optimization, payment processing, reputation management, revenue cycle

**Their sales approach:**
- Education-led (webinars, blog content, whitepapers)
- Position as "data-driven" vs. gut feeling pricing
- Recommend quarterly fee updates — keeps them top of mind
- One-time purchase removes subscription friction

**What Sikka does NOT do:**
- They don't look at your own historical prices to find stale ones
- They don't calculate what you're losing per day right now
- They don't implement anything — report only
- They don't have a .99 Factor or psychological pricing layer
- They don't serve Canadian clinics specifically (no Canadian zip code data mentioned)

**The Canadian gap:**
Sikka is built on US zip codes. The AbVMA fee guide and Canadian market data are completely outside their model. This is a home field advantage for Clinic IQ in Alberta and Canada broadly.

**How to position Clinic IQ against Sikka in a sales conversation:**

| | Sikka Fee Survey | Clinic IQ |
|--|--|--|
| What does it analyze? | What competitors charge | What YOU charge that's out of date |
| Data source | 1,700 external practices | Your own invoice history |
| Output | Market benchmarks | Prioritized stale price list + daily revenue loss |
| Implementation | None (report only) | Included (Jack's tool) |
| Pricing | $495 one-time | Subscription model |
| Canada | US only | Alberta-native |
| The .99 Factor | Not available | Exclusive |

**Bottom line on Sikka:** Respect them, don't fear them. They're solving the "am I priced correctly vs the market?" question. You're solving the "am I still charging 2021 prices in 2026?" question. A clinic could buy both — and many should.
